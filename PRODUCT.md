# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

adaptive

## Users

Pairs — spouses, couples, and close friends — deciding together what to eat, watch, or do. Context: phone in hand on the couch or in a group chat moment; both people swipe independently, often at different times. The job to be done: kill the "I don't know, what do you want?" loop by revealing only the options both people already like.

## Product Purpose

matchpoint is a Tinder-style swipe app for two people. Each partner swipes through curated decks in five categories — food, vacations, activities, date nights, and shows/movies — and the app reveals matches only when both swiped right. Success looks like: a pair goes from "no idea" to a shortlist of mutual yeses in under five minutes, with zero sign-up friction (join by 6-character invite code).

## Positioning

The only decision app where a "yes" is guaranteed to be a yes for both of you.

## Brand Personality

Warm, playful, celebratory. Date-night energy: rounded cards, springy swipe physics, a small moment of delight when a match lands. Fun without being loud — the app is the icebreaker, not the main event.

## Anti-references

- Tinder's dark neon dating-app aesthetic — this is for established pairs, not strangers.
- Gamified streak/points mechanics — no pressure, no scores.
- Generic AI-app scaffolding: cream/beige backgrounds, gradient hero text, eyebrow kickers on every section.

## Design Principles

1. **Two yeses or nothing.** Every screen reinforces mutuality — matches are the product; solo swipes are private.
2. **Zero-friction start.** Create → share code → swipe. No accounts, no email, no onboarding tour.
3. **Delight at the match, calm everywhere else.** Celebration is earned by a mutual yes; the rest of the surface stays quiet (Calm-Surface Rule).
4. **Thumb-first.** One-handed swipe ergonomics; every target ≥ 48px.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast in light and dark themes (Muted-Floor Rule: muted text ≥ 4.5:1). All swipe gestures have button equivalents (❌ / ❤️ buttons). Animations gated on `prefers-reduced-motion`.
